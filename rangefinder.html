Dorm Hallway Rangefinder

This project stemmed from classic dorm boredom. My floormates and I wanted to find a way to spice up the look of our floor for Christmastime besides the traditional lights. I led a team of three to build a custom controller and firmware to interface a Garmin LIDAR sensor to a long strip of NeoPixel LEDs run over our main hallway. This rangefinder would be able to calculate the distance of a moving body, and in turn light up the section of the strip corresponding to the body. This created a strip of light that could follow person down the hallway.

Our controller consisted of an AdaFruit Feather M4 and multiple breakout boards to interact with the LIDAR sensor. This board was chosen for it's good NeoPixel compatibility and integrated RTJ45 connectors.

I built a proof-of-concept using individual LEDs in Novemeber. Unfortunately, after failing to complete the project by Christmas, we lost some motivation. We finished a fully-functional prototype by early January, but decided not to put up the lights and recycle our custom controller for another project.
